You already live here. These are the people in your life and the situations you navigate together.

Chapters — Reworked

No "welcome to Germany" tutorials. You're already here. These are things that happen to you.

KAPITEL 1 — mit Leyla

Der Brief vom Amt

A confusing official letter arrives. Leyla comes over, translates the Beamtendeutsch, and rants about how the system works (and doesn't). You learn bureaucracy by surviving it.

LiD TOPICS

Meldepflicht, Behörden, Föderalismus, Amtssprache

KAPITEL 2 — mit Minh

Stolpersteine

Walking through Mitte, Minh stops at a Stolperstein and tells you about his grandparents' journey from Vietnam to the DDR. German history through the lens of someone whose family lived it.

LiD TOPICS

NS-Zeit, DDR, Wiedervereinigung, Erinnerungskultur

KAPITEL 3 — mit Olu

Krankenschein

You're sick. Olu walks you through the Krankenkasse system — public vs private, what's covered, how to call in sick. He's done this dance before and has stories.

LiD TOPICS

Sozialversicherung, Krankenversicherung, Pflegeversicherung

KAPITEL 4 — mit Franzi

Wahlkampf im Späti

Election posters are everywhere. Over beers at the Späti, Franzi explains Erst- und Zweitstimme, the parties, and why Berlin politics is its own circus. You debate. She gets heated.

LiD TOPICS

Bundestag, Wahlen, Parteien, Demokratie, Koalition

KAPITEL 5 — mit Jule

Demo am Samstag

Jule invites you to a protest in Friedrichshain. On the way, they explain Versammlungsfreiheit, Meinungsfreiheit, and what rights you actually have. Also: what the police can and can't do.

LiD TOPICS

Grundrechte Art. 1-19, Versammlungsfreiheit, Meinungsfreiheit, Rechtsstaat

KAPITEL 6 — mit Karim

Steuererklärung und Schmerz

Tax season. Karim is doing his Steuererklärung for the Imbiss and roping you in. Work contracts, Steuerklassen, Gewerkschaften — told through Karim's real experience building a business here.

LiD TOPICS

Steuern, Arbeitsrecht, Gewerkschaften, Gewerbefreiheit

KAPITEL 7 — mit Leyla

Ärger mit dem Vermieter

Your landlord is being shady. Leyla switches into lawyer mode — tenant rights, Mietvertrag clauses, where to complain. She's done this before for half of Neukölln.

LiD TOPICS

Grundrechte, Mietrecht, Verbraucherschutz, Rechtsweg

KAPITEL 8 — mit Jule

Kindergeburtstag Chaos

You help Jule organize a birthday party at the Kita. Conversations with parents reveal how Schulpflicht works, Kita vs Grundschule, and why everyone has opinions about the Bildungssystem.

LiD TOPICS

Bildungssystem, Schulpflicht, Erziehung, Jugendschutz

KAPITEL 9 — mit Minh & Franzi

WG-Abend: Europa und die Welt

A WG dinner turns into a debate about the EU, NATO, and Germany's role in the world. Minh brings the historical angle, Franzi the political theory. You try to keep up — in German.

LiD TOPICS

EU, NATO, UNO, Weimarer Republik, Grundgesetz history

KAPITEL 10 — alle zusammen

Karim's Imbiss-Eröffnung

Karim's Imbiss reopens after renovation. Everyone's there. The whole crew, the whole city. A celebration chapter that weaves together Berlin-specific LiD questions through conversations with everyone you've met.

LiD TOPICS

Berlin: Abgeordnetenhaus, Bezirke, Landeswappen, comprehensive review

Design philosophy: Nobody lectures you. You learn because Leyla is pissed about your landlord, because Minh gets emotional at a Stolperstein, because Franzi won't shut up about coalitions at the Späti. The LiD content is real — but the delivery is human, opinionated, sometimes messy. Just like Berlin.
