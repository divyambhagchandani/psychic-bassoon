Three Approaches for Berlin Leben

Pick the architecture that fits your month-long timeline best

A

Chapter-Driven Linear Path

Structure: 10-12 chapters, each a Berlin life scenario (Bürgeramt, Wohnung, Wahl...). Each chapter has a story intro → lesson content → mixed exercises → chapter quiz. Unlock next chapter by passing.

Adaptive: Failed questions enter a spaced-repetition review queue that appears at the start of each session.

Pros: Clear progression, easy to scope, strong narrative flow, natural stopping points.

Cons: Less flexible — you must follow the path. Review feels separate from story.

Build time: ~2-3 weeks for core, 1 week polish

B

Berlin Map Hub + Missions

Structure: A visual Berlin map as the home screen. Locations (Rathaus, Schule, Krankenhaus...) unlock missions. Each mission = a scenario with exercises. Daily review missions appear as "errands" on the map.

Adaptive: Weak areas resurface as "urgent errands" — spaced repetition baked into the map metaphor.

Pros: Non-linear exploration, the map IS the progress tracker, review feels organic. Very engaging visually.

Cons: More complex to build (map UI, location states). Harder to ensure full LiD coverage without linear ordering.

Build time: ~3-4 weeks, tight for a month

C

Hybrid: Linear Story + Daily Dashboard

Structure: Two modes — Story Mode (linear chapters, new content + narrative) and Tägliche Übung (daily practice dashboard with streak, review queue, quick drills). Story unlocks new content; daily practice reinforces it.

Adaptive: Daily practice is 100% driven by spaced repetition. Story mode advances when you're ready (based on review performance).

Pros: Best of both — narrative engagement + flexible daily practice. Adapts to 10min days (just do daily) vs 1hr days (daily + story). Clear separation of "learn new" vs "review".

Cons: Two UIs to build. Slightly more complexity than pure linear.

Build time: ~3 weeks core, but the two-mode split parallelizes well

⭐ Recommendation: Option C (Hybrid)

It naturally handles your variable schedule (short days = daily review, long days = story + review), keeps the narrative engaging, and the spaced repetition review is its own dedicated space rather than awkwardly mixed into the story. The two modes also parallelize well during development.
